Overview

This project involved the complete mechanical design, analysis, and documentation of a bevel gearbox system. Developed in collaboration with Gift Chidewu, this gearbox is designed to transmit rotational motion between perpendicular shafts with a 1.5:1 transmission ratio (16:24 teeth). The project combined comprehensive force calculations, shaft and bearing design, stress analysis, and practical manufacturing considerations to create a functional, cost-effective power transmission system.

Project Objectives

The primary objectives were to:

  • Design a complete bevel gearbox system meeting the specified 1.5:1 transmission ratio
  • Calculate contact forces between meshing gears and distribute loads to shafts and bearings
  • Perform detailed stress analysis on both driving and driven shafts
  • Select and verify appropriate bearings for radial and axial loading conditions
  • Design parallel key connections for torque transmission
  • Specify sealing and lubrication systems for durability
  • Create production-ready technical drawings and assembly documentation

Material Selection

Materials were carefully selected based on loading conditions, machinability, and cost-effectiveness:

  • Bevel Gears: Case-hardened steel (standard specification) — provides superior wear resistance and fatigue strength for gear teeth
  • Driving Shaft: C45 Steel — offers excellent balance of strength and machinability for moderate-speed applications
  • Driven Shaft: 42CrMo4 Steel — high-strength alloy steel providing enhanced load-carrying capacity and fatigue resistance
  • Bearings: High-carbon chromium steel (100Cr6) — standard material for rolling element bearings, providing hardness and durability
  • Housing: Cast Iron — selected for strength, vibration damping, heat dissipation, and ease of complex machining
  • Lubrication: Multi-purpose lithium-based grease — ideal for medium-speed applications with excellent thermal stability and water resistance
  • Sealing: Radial shaft seals and rubber lip seals — prevent contamination and retain lubricant while accommodating shaft rotation

Force Analysis & Calculations

Pitch Angles

For bevel gears with perpendicular shafts, pitch cone angles are determined by the gear ratio:

  • Driving Gear Pitch Angle (δ₁): arctan(16/24) = 33.69°
  • Driven Gear Pitch Angle (δ₂): 90° − 33.69° = 56.31°

Gear Contact Forces

Tangential force on the driving gear with 5.9 Nm torque and 48 mm pitch diameter:

  • Tangential Force (Ft): 245.83 N — primary load transmitted by gear teeth
  • Normal Force (Fn): Calculated using pressure angle α = 20°
  • Radial Force (Fr): 79.31 N — directed toward gear center
  • Axial Force (Fa): 70.76 N — along shaft axis due to cone angle

These calculated forces were used to size shafts, select bearings, and design support structures. The forces distribute differently to each bearing based on shaft geometry and bearing position.

Shaft Stress Analysis

Both shafts were analyzed under combined bending and torsional loading. The analysis verifies that stress levels remain safely below material yield strengths.

Driving Shaft (C45 Steel, 15 mm diameter, 100 mm length)

  • Bending Stress: 67.32 N/mm² — from radial gear forces acting at distance from supports
  • Torsional Stress: 16.41 N/mm² — from torque transmission
  • Von Mises Equivalent Stress: 75.09 N/mm² — combined stress criterion
  • Allowable Yield Strength (C45): 245 N/mm²
  • Safety Factor: >3.0 — excellent margin of safety

Driven Shaft (42CrMo4 Steel, 20 mm diameter, 150 mm length)

  • Bending Stress: 48.04 N/mm² — lower than driving shaft due to larger diameter
  • Torsional Stress: 5.98 N/mm² — proportional to lower output torque
  • Von Mises Equivalent Stress: 51.65 N/mm² — well within safe limits
  • Allowable Yield Strength (42CrMo4): 500 N/mm²
  • Safety Factor: >9.5 — substantial safety margin

Both shafts demonstrate adequate strength. The driven shaft's higher allowable strength combined with its larger diameter provides significant safety margin despite longer length and higher bending moment.

Bearing Selection & Verification

Four bearings were selected based on calculated loads, shaft positions, and operational requirements. A combination of tapered roller bearings for fixed supports (near gears) and deep groove ball bearings for floating supports (away from gears) was implemented.

Driving Shaft Bearings

Bearing A (Fixed Support) — Tapered Roller Bearing 30302

  • Position: Close to driving gear, handles combined axial and radial loads
  • Bore Diameter: 15 mm
  • Outside Diameter: 42 mm
  • Width: 14.25 mm
  • Radial Load (Fr): 40.74 N
  • Axial Load (Fa): 49.63 N
  • Equivalent Dynamic Load: 131.63 N
  • Bearing Life: 2,177,648.7 hours — provides long service life
  • Dynamic Load Rating (C): 19.3 kN — safely accommodates all loads

Bearing B (Floating Support) — Deep Groove Ball Bearing 7302b

  • Position: Support away from gear, primarily radial loading
  • Bore Diameter: 15 mm
  • Outside Diameter: 42 mm
  • Width: 13 mm
  • Radial Load: 115 N
  • Equivalent Dynamic Load: 85 N
  • Bearing Life: 5,278,716.9 hours — allows free axial movement
  • Purpose: Floating support prevents preloading from thermal expansion

Parallel Key Connections

Parallel keys transmit torque between shafts and gear hubs while allowing for accurate positioning. All key dimensions follow DIN 6558 specifications.

Driving Shaft Key (5 × 5 × 20 mm)

  • Shaft Diameter: 15 mm
  • Key Cross-Section: 5 × 5 mm
  • Shaft Groove Depth: 3 mm
  • Hub Groove Depth: 2.3 mm
  • Shaft Material: C45 Steel (Re ≈ 245 N/mm²)
  • Hub Material: 42CrMo4 Steel (Re ≈ 500 N/mm²)
  • Permissible Pressure (shaft): 163.33 N/mm²
  • Permissible Pressure (hub): 250 N/mm²

  • Safety Margin: Adequate for secure torque transmission

  • Safety Factor: >5 — substantial safety margin

Housing, Sealing & Lubrication

Housing Design

Material: Cast Iron — selected for its excellent combination of properties:

  • High strength and rigidity to support all internal loads
  • Excellent vibration damping — reduces noise and improves durability
  • Superior heat dissipation — maintains optimal operating temperature
  • Corrosion resistance — ensures longevity in various environments
  • Ease of machining and complex geometry — allows production of mounting surfaces and bearing bores with required tolerances

Lubrication System

Lubricant: Multi-purpose lithium-based grease — selected for medium-speed, moderate-load applications:

  • Excellent thermal stability — maintains consistent performance across temperature range
  • Superior water resistance — prevents degradation in humid environments
  • Strong adhesion to metal surfaces — ensures retention on moving components
  • Low maintenance requirements — operates reliably with minimal service intervals
  • Ideal for enclosed gearbox systems — prevents contamination while providing continuous lubrication

Sealing System

Critical to maintaining system reliability and longevity:

  • Radial Shaft Seals: Prevent dirt, moisture, and debris from entering the housing
  • Rubber Lip Seals: Integrated around shafts for effective contamination exclusion
  • Function: Retain lubricant inside the housing while protecting internal components from external environment
  • Result: Enhanced overall efficiency and extended bearing and gear service life

Design Verification & Manufacturing Readiness

Interference Check

SolidWorks CAD assembly analysis confirmed zero component interferences. All parts fit within designed tolerances, with adequate clearances for assembly and operation. The design is verified to be manufacturable without modifications.

Technical Drawing Documentation

Complete production-ready drawings include:

  • Detailed shaft drawings with all dimensions, tolerances, and surface finishes
  • Assembly drawings showing all components in their correct positions
  • Sectional views for clarity on internal component arrangement
  • Bending moment diagrams validating structural adequacy
  • All drawings follow DIN and ISO standards for technical documentation

Results & Conclusions

This bevel gearbox design successfully demonstrates the integration of theoretical engineering knowledge with practical design considerations. The project achieved all stated objectives:

  • Gear Design: Achieves the specified 1.5:1 transmission ratio with 16 and 24 teeth configuration
  • Force Distribution: All calculated contact forces distributed appropriately to shaft and bearing supports
  • Shaft Design: Both driving (15 mm, C45) and driven (20 mm, 42CrMo4) shafts provide safety factors >3.0 against yield
  • Bearing Selection: Four bearing types (two tapered roller, two ball/angular contact) chosen for specific load conditions with life calculations exceeding 2 million hours
  • Key Connections: Both parallel key designs verified with Method C calculations showing adequate loaded length and safe pressure distribution
  • Manufacturing: Simple, cost-effective cast iron housing design with precision-machined bearing bores ensures assemblability
  • Lubrication & Sealing: Multi-purpose lithium grease and radial seals selected for long-term reliability in enclosed system
  • Documentation: Complete technical drawings provide clear manufacturing guidance and assembly instructions
Final Gearbox Design

The design balances mechanical performance with manufacturability and cost-effectiveness, making it suitable for production. The iterative process of calculation, design refinement, and verification demonstrates professional engineering practice in mechanical power transmission design.
